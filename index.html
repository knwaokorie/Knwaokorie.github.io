Transforming the Tougaloo College Digital Campus Maps

Abstract

In 1869, the American Missiona an ury Association of New York purchased five hundred acres of land that had long been known as the Boddie Plantation to establish a school for the training of young people born into slavery.

The Boddie Plantation Research Project was undertaken to uncover the historical evidence of the Boddie Plantation and the surrounding community to create a digital archive that documents the history, slave culture, and society of that period. The project also intends to document the transition from the plantation into the early days of Tougaloo College.

A Geographical Information System (GIS) will be used to build the foundation for the historical map of the Boddie Plantation. The first step was to digitize the current campus map. GIS is a program that is used to make map layers which links every item to attributes in a database. Maps in GIS are never one dimensional, but instead are a dynamic representation of geographically based data. The original campus map was created utilizing QGIS open source software and Google maps. The purpose of this project is to add historical layers from 1916 and 1884 and to test the effectiveness of the digital campus map through statistical analysis and feedback in order to better the campus map and implement it in every day use.

Narrative Statement

It’s 5:30 in the morning. Across the top of a Word document was the question, “What is your dream career? Why? How do you plan to achieve it?” This was an assignment given by my 8th grade English teacher that was due that day during third period. Underneath the prompt was less than a paragraph, not due to procrastination but instead because I simply didn’t know what my dream career was. Unfortunately, I was unable to change the state of my essay prior to my third block class and was forced to present my three sentences. After class, the teacher pulled me aside and asked why I didn’t complete the assignment. When I explained to her my reasoning, she said to me, “Kingsley, take something that you love doing that others would consider unprofessional and make it professional. That’s your dream career.” As a youth, I had exceptional typing skills, proficient in various programs, and I had made a couple of gaming websites of my own. I have always seen computers as a way to promote efficiency and serve as expressive platforms, but overall enhance the human life experience. My English teacher still gave me a failing grade on the assignment, but I was a step closer to discovering my dream career.
